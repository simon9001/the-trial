Early Life

Sheila Wambui, lovingly referred to as Wambo, was born in February 22nd, 1993 to her loving parents, Nancy and Francis Muiruri. Sheila was born with cerebral palsy, a condition that made her totally dependent on others for basic activities of daily living like feeding and grooming. To her loving parents, Wambo was never defined by her condition ,but by her radiant spirit, her smile and the unconditional love she gave and received. Hers was a story of resilience, warmth and quiet triumph.

Wambo was a loving sister to her siblings, Newton and Ian who adored her dearly. Together they shared laughter, playful moments and a bond that words can hardly capture. They delighted in her company,finding joy in her radiant smile and in the sound of her laughter that often filled their home whenever she was around. Her parents and siblings wrapped her in unwavering affection, creating a home where she was not only cared for, but celebrated.

Her Journey

Wambui was baptized at PCEA Banana, Kiambu, at her infancy, a moment that marked her as a child of Faith and surrounded her life with grace and divine protection.That Faith became the gentle light guiding her and her family through every step of her journey.

From a tender age,Wambo began her occupational therapy journey, a path that demanded strength far beyond her years. Session after session, she faced each challenge with quiet courage and a spirit that refused to give up. Her parents stood faithfully by her side through it all, never missing a single appointment, never letting her walk that journey alone. They cheered her on during every stretch, every movement, and every small milestone, their love becoming the steady rhythm that carried her forward.

Wambo spent some of her life in special schools such as SCOM Disabled Children’s Home and Training Center in Ongata Rongai, surrounded by dedicated caregivers who nurtured her growth and honored her individuality.When the school holidays came, Wambo would go home to the embrace of her family, where joy overflowed in every moment spent together.

Sadly,on Tuesday 21st October 2025, after a brief illness, our Baby Gal’s journey on earth ended and took a flight into eternal rest .

Her Memory

Wambui taught us all something profound; that joy can exist in any circumstance, that love is the greatest gift we can give, and that a life lived with laughter and connection is a life well lived. Even if her body often limited her, her heart knew no limits. She lived with cerebral palsy,but it never defined her. What defined her was her radiant smile and the boundless love she gave and received. She was a jovial soul always ready with a giggle, a sparkle in her eyes, and a warmth that made everyone feel seen and cherished. Though her physical presence is no longer with us, her spirit remains etched in our hearts, in our memories, and in the stories we will continue to tell. She was a light that will never dim.

Legacy

To those who cared for Wambo, thank you so so so much. Your compassion, patience, and devotion gave her a life full of dignity and delight. You were her champions, her protectors, and her friends. May God richly bless you. Rest in eternal peace dear Baby Gal. You were deeply loved and will be forever missed.

Born with cerebral palsy that rendered her totally dependent on others for care, Sheila remained the baby of the family for over 32 years. Our precious gift, our gentle joy, our constant reminder of love’s power.

My dear little sister, Wambo,

Many times when I visited you in school, you would sense our presence from far, and you would scream with the excitement of seeing your loved ones — which made us always look forward to the next visit — oh, and of course with your favorite drink, Delamere yoghurt.

Though you could not speak to us with words, you always found your own way to communicate — through your radiant smile, your laughter, and the light in your eyes. We always had a way we communicated and understood each other.

I remember those moments when you would protest whenever we tried to change your TV program at home, because you knew exactly what you wanted — and you made sure we followed your lead — and we loved you all the more for it.

On that very day which remains vivid, we saw you from a distance — quiet, peaceful — and for a moment, we waited to hear your familiar scream of excitement, the one that always filled our hearts whenever we came to visit. But this time, there was silence. You were already resting, deeply asleep in God’s loving arms.

Today, as we say goodbye, our hearts are full of gratitude. We thank God for the beautiful years He allowed us to share with you. The laughter we shared, the talks, and the quiet moments in between are memories I’ll hold close forever. You had a way of making everything feel lighter.

We shall miss you dearly, our baby girl.

Sleep peacefully in God’s embrace.
